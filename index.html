Rizal's Literary Masterpieces

The two novels that exposed colonial abuses and awakened Filipino consciousness

Noli Me Tangere

"Touch Me Not"

Published 1887 Berlin, Germany First Novel

Plot Summary

The story follows Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra, a young Filipino who returns to his homeland after studying in Europe. He discovers the corruption and abuses of the Spanish colonial government and the Catholic Church, particularly through the character of Padre Dámaso, a corrupt friar.

Key Characters

• Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra - Protagonist
• María Clara - Love interest
• Padre Dámaso - Antagonist friar
• Elías - Ibarra's mysterious friend

El Filibusterismo

"The Reign of Greed"

Published 1891 Ghent, Belgium Sequel

Plot Summary

The sequel to Noli Me Tangere, this darker novel follows Simoun (Ibarra in disguise) as he returns to the Philippines seeking revenge against the colonial system. The story depicts the consequences of colonial oppression and the growing revolutionary sentiment.

Key Characters

• Simoun (Ibarra) - Protagonist
• Basilio - Medical student
• Isagani - Idealistic student
• Paulita Gómez - Isagani's love
