Interactive Timeline 35 Years of Impact National Hero
1861

Birth in Calamba

June 19, 1861

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born in Calamba, Laguna, to Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso.

Learn more about his early life
1872-1877

Ateneo Municipal

Secondary Education

Rizal studied at the Ateneo Municipal de Manila, where he excelled academically and graduated with highest honors.

Learn about his education
1877-1882

University of Santo Tomas

Higher Education in Manila

Rizal enrolled at the University of Santo Tomas, studying medicine and philosophy, but grew disillusioned with the colonial education system.

Explore his university years
1882-1887

European Education

Studies in Spain and Beyond

Rizal traveled to Europe to continue his medical studies and was exposed to liberal ideas that shaped his political thinking. During this time, he wrote his first novel, Noli Me Tangere.

Discover his European journey
1887-1892

Return to Philippines

Medical Practice and Advocacy

Rizal returned to the Philippines and practiced medicine while continuing his advocacy for reforms. He founded the La Liga Filipina and wrote El Filibusterismo.

1892-1896

Exile in Dapitan

Four Years of Service

Exiled to Dapitan, Zamboanga del Norte, Rizal continued his medical practice, taught students, and contributed to the community's development through various projects.

1896

Martyrdom

December 30, 1896

Rizal was executed by firing squad at Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park) in Manila, after being convicted of rebellion. His martyrdom inspired the Philippine Revolution.
